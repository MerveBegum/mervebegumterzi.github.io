About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the National Magnetic Resonance Research Center (UMRAM) & the Department of Electrical & Electronics Engineering at Bilkent University, under the supervision of Prof. Emine Ulku Saritas.

I hold both a Ph.D. & an M.S. in Electrical & Electronics Engineering from Bilkent University, where I was advised by Prof. Orhan Arikan for both degrees.

My research spans a broad interdisciplinary spectrum at the nexus of signal processing, machine learning, & medical imaging. Over the years, I have worked on diverse projects involving anomaly detection, image processing & reconstruction, computer vision, pattern recognition & clinical decision support systems.

I specialize in developing data-efficient learning approaches—including unsupervised, self-supervised, weakly-supervised, & few-shot learning to address challenges associated with limited annotated datasets. My expertise also extends to inverse problem solving, federated learning & explainable AI (XAI).

Through interdisciplinary collaboration & a strong engineering foundation, I aim to translate theoretical machine learning & signal processing advances into tangible improvements in next-generation medical imaging & intelligent health systems.
