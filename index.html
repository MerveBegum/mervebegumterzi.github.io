About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the National Magnetic Resonance Research Center (UMRAM) & the Department of Electrical & Electronics Engineering at Bilkent University, working under the supervision of Prof. Emine Ulku Saritas. I hold a Ph.D. & an M.S. in Electrical & Electronics Engineering from Bilkent University, where I was advised by Prof. Orhan Arikan for both degrees.

My research focuses on the intersection of machine learning, medical imaging & signal processing. Specifically, I design & implement novel generative models for medical image reconstruction & anomaly detection frameworks for automated disease diagnosis, with applications in Magnetic Particle Imaging (MPI), Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) & Computed Tomography (CT).

My work emphasizes data-efficient learning approaches such as unsupervised, self-supervised, weakly-supervised, and few-shot learning, addressing the challenge of limited annotated data through innovative inverse problem solving techniques.

Through interdisciplinary collaboration & a strong engineering foundation, I aim to translate theoretical machine learning & signal processing advances into tangible improvements in medical imaging & diagnostic decision support systems.
